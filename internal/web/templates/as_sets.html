{{template "icon-info"}}What an AS set is for

BIRD has no idea what an IRR AS-SET is. When a customer hands you AS-CUSTOMER, it has to be expanded — with bgpq4 — into the list of member AS numbers before the router can filter on it. That list is what lives here.

An import policy then requires every route’s origin AS — the last hop of its AS path — to be a member. That is the origin half of a customer filter; the prefix set is the other half, saying which prefixes may come from those origins.

{{if .Bgpq4}}

Give a set its AS-SET name and birdy runs the expansion for you: Expand from IRR on the form fills the members in, and auto-refresh keeps them current on a schedule. Refreshes only update the model — they show up under Changes for you to review and apply.

{{else}}

Start birdy with --bgpq4 bgpq4 to expand and auto-refresh these members from the IRR instead of pasting them by hand.
